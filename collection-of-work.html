Brain on Fire

The idea for this project came to me at my cottage, where I remembered fireworks and sparklers from the previous years Canada day. One night, I went to the darkest place on the property, lit up sparkles and performed various movements to create symmetrical images. The results were terrific - I got many interesting photos - but this image was the best. It immediately caught my attention as it reminded me of the image of the human brain firing neurons. I am very happy with the final result.

Gray Day

I made this piece called “Gray Day” for a photography assignment on grids. Our task was to create a grid collage of a certain theme while staying inside the school. I took various images of the building and classrooms, but ultimately, I decided to create a collage featuring another student in my class. I made various shots of him around school, and while doing it, I decided to incorporate the mask to highlight that the work was done during the pandemic. The mask also allowed me to express further the mood of that day, which was sad, dark and wet.

Low Key Tech

This image, "Low Key Tech" is a delightful exploration into low-key photography with a laptop as the subject. Rapidly, I discovered the nuanced artistry of low-key techniques, particularly in the context of inanimate objects. Experimenting with various angles and lighting setups, I sought to capture the essence of the laptop in a compelling manner. Employing subtle adjustments and creative flair, I accentuated the laptop's features to evoke a sense of mystery and allure. After several shots, I finally achieved the desired effect, where the laptop emerged as a captivating focal point amidst the shadows.

Negative Space

"Negative Space" is a photograph that holds significant meaning for me, capturing the essence of exploring negative space within art. Taken at a museum, the image features a striking statue enveloped in shadow, with the surrounding emptiness serving as the focal point. Through this image, I aimed to evoke a sense of wonder and introspection, prompting appreciation for the beauty of simplicity and the power of suggestion in artistic expression.

High Key Duo

This image, “High Key,” is another one of my favourites. I really enjoyed experimenting with different equipment to create high and low-key photos. Very quickly, I realized that high-key ptotography was a great way to portray people. I asked two students from my class to step into the shot and try different poses together. After several attempts, I realized that although the images looked good, they lacked interest. I asked one of the students to add some paint to his face to make it stand out. After about three shots, I knew I had got the right one.
